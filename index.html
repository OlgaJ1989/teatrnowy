Sussex Walks

About Sussex

Imagine valleys covered with a lush carpet of green grass, stretching all the way to the endless expanse of the sea on one side and rolling hills on the other. Now imagine you are there, walking and taking it all in…

Maybe it’s not your cup of tea and you would much rather prefer a walk through a vibrant town, bustling with noise and movement, showcasing some of the best-known pieces of architecture in the UK.

Whatever you are looking for in a walk, Sussex has you covered. Here are our top walks in Brighton and the surrounding area.

Popular Walks

The Undercliff Walk

This 4-kilometre-long route will take you from the Brighton Marina to Saltdean, showcasing a pebbled beach on one side and white chalk cliffs on the other. It starts at the marina situated about a kilometre and a half east from the town centre and takes you past places such as Rottingdean and Rodean, before finishing at Saltdean.

Brighton to Rottingdean

This clifftop path will take you from the Brighton Pier to Rottingdean, showcasing magnificent views of the sea and leading past the Beacon Mill, an old smock windmill dating back to 1802. Once you reach Rottingdean, you will be greeted by some charming cafes and pubs, some of them occupying 400 year old buildings.

Exceat to Birling Gap

To get to the starting point of this walk you will need to take a short car or bus ride from Brighton to Exceat. From there, the walk will take you through the Seven Sisters Country Park where you will be able to admire and conquer the famous cliffs on your way to Birling Gap. The walk takes approximately an hour and a half.

Brighton to Shoreham

This town walk starts at the iconic Brighton Palace Pier and takes you all the way to a neighbouring town of Shoreham. On the way you will see such landmarks as the famous West Pier and examples of regency architecture such as the Regency Square. You will also experience the hustle and bustle of a busy seaside resort.
